Planning

Short Term Goals
  1. For developing tailored educational materials and reminders for medication adherence:
    • Collaborate with healthcare providers, educators, and patients to identify specific educational needs and preferences.
    • Utilize digital platforms or apps to create personalized educational materials and reminders based on patient preferences and health literacy levels.
    • Provide easy access to resources through online portals, mobile apps, or printed materials.
    • Implement feedback mechanisms to continuously improve the effectiveness of educational materials and reminders.
  2. To implement regular screening and monitoring for diabetes-related complications:
    • Develop standardized protocols based on evidence-based guidelines for screening and monitoring various complications associated with diabetes
    • Utilize electronic health records (EHR) or clinical decision support systems (CDSS) to automate reminders for screening appointments and tests.
    • Coordinate care between healthcare providers to ensure timely follow-up and appropriate management of identified complications.
    • Educate patients about the importance of regular screenings and empower them to take an active role in their healthcare management.
  3. For initiating a structured diabetes education program tailored to patient learning needs
    • Assess each patient's learning preferences, health literacy level, cultural background, and language proficiency to tailor the education program accordingly.
    • Offer a variety of educational formats such as individual counseling sessions, group classes, online modules, or multimedia resources to accommodate diverse learning styles.
    • Provide access to certified diabetes educators or support groups who can address specific concerns and provide ongoing guidance.
    • Monitor patient progress and adjust the education program as needed to ensure comprehension and retention of essential information.
Long Term Goals
  1. Establish sustainable systems for ongoing patient education and support:
    • Secure funding and resources to maintain educational programs and support services over time.
    • Foster partnerships with community organizations, advocacy groups, and healthcare stakeholders to expand reach and sustainability.
    • Evaluate the effectiveness of education and support initiatives through outcome measures and patient feedback, making adjustments as necessary.
  2. Institutionalize regular screening and monitoring protocols for diabetes-related complications:
    • Integrate screening and monitoring protocols into routine clinical practice guidelines and workflows.
    • Provide training and education to healthcare providers on the importance of regular screenings and adherence to protocols.
    • Utilize quality improvement initiatives to ensure consistency and adherence to screening guidelines across healthcare settings.
  3. Integrate diabetes education programs as a standard part of patient care, with continuous improvement based on feedback and outcomes:
    • Embed diabetes education programs into the standard care pathways for patients diagnosed with diabetes.
    • Incorporate patient feedback and outcomes data into program evaluation and quality improvement efforts.
    • Continuously update educational materials and curriculum based on emerging research, best practices, and patient needs.
